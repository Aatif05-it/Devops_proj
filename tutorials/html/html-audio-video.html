HTML Audio & Video

HTML lets you embed sound and video directly on a page using the <audio> and <video> elements with builtâ€‘in controls.

Basic audio player

The simplest audio element needs a src and the controls attribute so users can play or pause. 

<audio src="sound.mp3" controls>
  Your browser does not support the audio element.
</audio>

Audio with multiple sources

Use nested <source> tags to provide different formats (for example MP3 and OGG) for better browser support. 

<audio controls>
  <source src="sound.mp3" type="audio/mpeg">
  <source src="sound.ogg" type="audio/ogg">
  Your browser does not support the audio element.
</audio>

Basic video player

The <video> element works like audio but can also have width, height, and a poster image shown before playback. 

<video width="640" height="360" controls>
  <source src="movie.mp4" type="video/mp4">
  <source src="movie.webm" type="video/webm">
  Your browser does not support the video tag.
</video>

Common attributes

Useful attributes for audio and video include controls, autoplay, loop, and muted. Autoplay is often required to be muted to work in modern browsers. 

<video src="intro.mp4" controls autoplay muted loop></video>
