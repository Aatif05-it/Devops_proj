SQL Normalization

Normalization eliminates data redundancy and dependency by organizing tables and relationships, typically through 1NF, 2NF, and 3NF forms.

It prevents update anomalies while maintaining data integrity through proper primary and foreign key relationships.

1NF: First Normal Form

Eliminate repeating groups; every column must contain atomic (single) values with a primary key. 

-- UNNORMALIZED (repeating groups)
CustomerOrders
CustomerName | Products
"John"       | "Book, Pen, Notebook"
"Mary"       | "Laptop"

-- 1NF (atomic values)
CustomerOrders
CustomerID | OrderID | ProductName
1          | 101     | Book
1          | 101     | Pen
1          | 101     | Notebook

1NF enables efficient querying and prevents inconsistent multi-value storage.

2NF: Eliminate Partial Dependencies

Remove dependencies on only part of a composite primary key by creating separate tables. 

-- 1NF but violates 2NF
OrderItems
OrderID | ProductID | ProductName | CustomerName
101     | 1         | Book        | John
101     | 2         | Pen         | John

-- 2NF (separate tables)
Orders
OrderID | CustomerID
101     | 1

OrderItems
OrderID | ProductID
101     | 1
101     | 2

Products
ProductID | ProductName
1         | Book
2         | Pen

ProductName now stored once; changing a product name updates all orders automatically.

3NF: Eliminate Transitive Dependencies

Remove dependencies between non-key columns by isolating independent entities. 

-- Violates 3NF (DeptLocation depends on DeptName)
Employees
EmpID | EmpName | DeptName | DeptLocation
1     | John    | Sales    | Mumbai
2     | Mary    | Sales    | Mumbai

-- 3NF
Employees
EmpID | EmpName | DeptID
1     | John    | 1
2     | Mary    | 1

Departments
DeptID | DeptName | Location
1      | Sales    | Mumbai

Department location changes in one place, not scattered across employee records.

Denormalization trade-offs

Purposefully add redundancy for read performance in data warehouses or reporting tables. 

-- Denormalized reporting table (fast reads)
CustomerOrderSummary
CustomerID | CustomerName | TotalOrders | LifetimeValue
1          | John         | 15          | 12500.00

Denormalization improves query speed but requires careful maintenance to avoid inconsistencies.
