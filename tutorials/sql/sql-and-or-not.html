SQL AND, OR, NOT

Logical operators AND, OR, and NOT combine WHERE conditions so you can express complex filters in one query.

AND requires all conditions to be true, OR requires at least one to be true, and NOT inverts a condition.

AND and OR together

This query finds customers in France or Germany whose city is Paris. Parentheses control the evaluation order. 

SELECT CustomerName, City, Country
FROM Customers
WHERE Country IN ('France', 'Germany')
  AND City = 'Paris';

Without parentheses, mixing AND and OR can change the meaning of the query, so grouping related conditions is important.

Using NOT to exclude rows

NOT is helpful when you want the opposite of a condition, such as excluding a status or a numeric range. 

-- Orders that are NOT between 100 and 500
SELECT OrderID, TotalAmount
FROM Orders
WHERE NOT (TotalAmount BETWEEN 100 AND 500);
